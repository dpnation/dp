04 — Voice & Tone

Brand Voice

dpnation speaks with the confidence of someone who knows their craft — and the warmth of someone who actually wants to help.

Primary tagline
it's more than a look,
it's a lifestyle
⚡

Direct

No corporate fluff, no filler. Every word earns its place. State the thing, then move on.

🎯

Expert

Speaks from a place of deep craft. Fluent in web design, UX, testing, and brand. The work speaks for itself.

✦

Warm

Not cold or clinical. There's a person here. "Welcome, friend" — not "Hello, visitor."

🏴

Intentional

Everything is considered. The lowercase, the spacing, the silence. Design is the message.

✓ Do

  • Write in lowercase for brand-level copy (taglines, nav, logo)
  • Use short sentences. Let them breathe.
  • Lead with the benefit or the work
  • Say "friend" not "user" — personal, not transactional
  • Describe craft with specificity (web design, UX, brand management)
  • Skip the Oxford comma in lists (e.g. "web design, UX and brand")

✕ Don't

  • Use buzzwords: "passionate," "synergy," "guru"
  • Over-explain or hedge unnecessarily
  • Use exclamation points in formal contexts
  • Use the brand color (Spark) for large areas of text
  • Write in second person for the primary tagline
  • Use em dashes — rewrite the sentence or use a comma instead
